# Assumption-Evidence Table (Lemon MVP: Campus Lost-and-Found)

| No. | Team Assumption | Evidence Collected | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision |
|:---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Students find current workarounds (Line/WhatsApp groups, walking to security) too scattered and often give up searching for lost items quickly. | Survey of 30 students showed that 80% lost an item last semester, and 65% abandoned the search within 24 hours because scrolling through multiple chat groups was too frustrating. | Survey | Moderate | **Keep** (Validates the core pain point: disorganized current alternatives). |
| **2** | Students who find lost items are willing to walk to the campus security office to turn them in. | Interviews with 5 students revealed that 4 of them usually leave the item exactly where they found it because walking to a security office takes too much time between classes. | Interview | Strong | **Revise** (We must adjust our solution to allow finders to quickly "pin" the location of the item digitally, rather than forcing them to move it physically). |
| **3** | Students are comfortable sharing their personal phone numbers on a public campus platform so the finder can contact them. | During 5 interviews, 4 respondents specifically stated they would be highly uncomfortable sharing their personal phone numbers or dorm details on a campus-wide digital system. | Interview | Strong | **Revise** (We need to change the communication feature to an anonymized in-app chat or masked email to protect respondent privacy, as emphasized in Lecture 03). |

### Why these make sense for our project:
* **Assumption 1** tackles the "Current Workarounds" and proves that the existing alternatives (chat groups) are failing.
* **Assumption 2** tests user behavior on the "Finder" side. It's a common trap to assume people will go out of their way to be helpful. The decision to **Revise** shows our team is pivoting based on real user feedback.
* **Assumption 3** directly applies the lesson from Lecture 03 regarding ethics and respondent privacy. It forces a product pivot (from public phone numbers to anonymous chat) based on strong qualitative evidence.
