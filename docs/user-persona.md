# Lab 04 - User Persona

## 1. Persona Name
**Justin, First Year University Student**

## 2. User Type
Student who frequently loses or finds personal items on campus.

## 3. Background and Context
- Experiences the problem while attending classes, studying in the library, or participating in campus activities.
- The problem usually happens when moving between different campus locations or after busy class schedules.
- Uses LINE, Facebook groups, social media, and university communication platforms to search for or report lost items.

## 4. Goals
- Find lost items as quickly as possible.
- Report lost or found items through one centralized platform.
- Receive updates when a matching item is found.
- Reduce the time spent searching across multiple communication channels.

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Information scattered across multiple channels | R2, R5, R8, R11 | Students reported checking several platforms before finding information. |
| Time-consuming search process | R1, R4, R7, R10 | Respondents said searching for lost items took too much time. |
| Unclear reporting procedure | R3, R6, R9 | Respondents were unsure where to report lost items. |
| No centralized tracking system | R12, S1 | Student and security staff confirmed there is no shared tracking system. |
| Low visibility of reports | R5, R13 | Social media posts were easily buried among other posts. |

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Asking friends or classmates | Limited reach and unreliable information | Multiple respondents |
| LINE/Facebook groups | Posts become buried and difficult to track | Multiple respondents |
| Social media posts | No centralized record and easy to miss | Multiple respondents |
| Visiting the security office | Time-consuming and requires physical visits | Multiple respondents |
| Asking teachers or staff | Information is not shared across all departments | Multiple respondents |

## 7. Design Implications
- The system should provide a centralized platform for reporting and searching lost items.
- The system should support both lost-item and found-item reports with search, filtering, and photo uploads.
- The team should avoid complicated reporting steps and relying on multiple communication channels.
- **Most important MVP need:** A simple, centralized system that allows students to quickly report, search, and track lost items.
