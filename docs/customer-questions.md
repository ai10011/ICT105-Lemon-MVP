# Customer Discovery Interview Guide

## Project Overview

This interview guide was developed as part of the **ICT105 Course Project** to conduct customer discovery research. The objective is to understand the real experiences, challenges, and current behaviors of university students regarding lost and found items on campus.

The insights gathered from these interviews will help identify user pain points and validate whether a digital solution would effectively address this problem.

---

# Research Objective

The purpose of this interview is to:

- Understand how students experience losing personal belongings on campus.
- Identify existing methods students use to recover lost items.
- Discover pain points, frustrations, and inefficiencies in the current process.
- Validate whether the identified problem is significant enough to justify developing a digital solution.

---

# Research Method

**Research Type:** Customer Discovery Interview

**Target Participants:** Undergraduate university students and Campus security staff and facility processing clerks

**Interview Format:** Semi-structured interview

**Estimated Duration:** 5–10 minutes

**Data Collection Method:**
- Interview notes
- Optional Google Form responses
- Anonymous qualitative feedback

---

# Interview Questions

## Section 1 — Consent and Introduction

1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT105 course project?

2. Your participation is completely voluntary. We will only collect information relevant to this research, and your identity will remain anonymous.

3. Do you consent to participating in this interview and allowing us to take notes?

---

## Section 2 — Understanding the User's Experience

4. Can you describe the most recent time you lost an item on campus or had difficulty locating a lost item?

5. What were you trying to accomplish at that moment?

6. Could you walk us through the entire experience, from when you realized the item was missing until the situation was resolved?

---

## Section 3 — Current Solutions and Behaviors

7. How do you currently deal with lost items on campus?

8. What resources do you usually rely on?
   - Friends
   - University staff
   - Security office
   - Social media groups
   - Messaging applications
   - Other methods

9. What aspects of the current process do you find difficult, slow, confusing, or frustrating?

---

## Section 4 — Problem Validation

10. How frequently do you experience this problem?

- Rarely
- Occasionally
- Frequently
- Very Frequently

11. On a scale of **1–5**, how serious do you consider this problem?

Please explain the reason for your rating.

12. What are the consequences if the lost item cannot be recovered?

Examples may include:
- Financial loss
- Loss of important documents
- Emotional stress
- Time wasted
- Academic disruption

---

## Section 5 — Closing Questions

13. If you could improve the current lost-and-found process, what changes would you suggest?

14. Is there anything else you think we should understand about this issue?

15. May we use your anonymous responses as supporting evidence in our ICT105 project report?

---

# Participant Information

- Participation is voluntary.

- You may skip any question or stop the interview at any time.

- Your responses will remain anonymous.

---

# What We Hope to Learn

From these interviews, we hope to understand:

- How students deal with lost items on campus.

- The biggest challenges in the current process.

- What students need from a better lost-and-found system.
