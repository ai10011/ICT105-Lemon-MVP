# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title
Campus Lost-and-Found System

## Target Users Studied
* **Students:** Primary users who frequently lose or find items on campus.
* **Security/Admin Staff:** Personnel responsible for handling lost-item reports and item returns.

## Number of Responses / Interviews
* **Students:** 13 Actual (12 Planned)
* **Security/Admin Staff:** 2 Actual (3 Planned)
* **Total Interviews:** 15 Completed

## Main Repeated Problem
There is no single centralized platform for reporting and searching for lost items on campus. Information is highly fragmented across disconnected channels, causing reports to be missed easily, creating confusion about where to report or check for updates, and wasting significant user time.

## Main Current Workarounds
* **Students:** Asking friends/classmates, posting in LINE/Facebook groups, publishing individual social media posts, making physical visits to the security office, and asking departmental teachers/staff.
* **Security/Admin Staff:** Keeping manual, paper-based record books that are difficult to search and manage efficiently.

## Strongest Evidence Found
* **High-Frequency Pain Points:** "Time-consuming search process" received **11 mentions** and "Information scattered across multiple channels" received **10 mentions**, both categorized as High severity.
* **Severe Consequences:** Strong evidence of recurring financial loss (replacing unrecovered items), emotional stress/frustration, and delayed access to campus services caused by lost ID/access cards.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings
* **Dependence on Community Participation:** Received only **3 mentions** (Medium severity) regarding whether system effectiveness will be throttled by passive user behavior.
* **Staff Workload Impact:** Rated as Moderate evidence strength due to a slightly lower sample size of admin staff interviewed (2 actual vs. 3 planned), though manual log inefficiency was fully confirmed.

## What We Must Revise
The problem direction is heavily validated, but the scope of the solution must be refined. Instead of a generic tracking system, the MVP must explicitly prioritize and optimize workflows for high-frequency, high-impact campus essentials: **Student IDs, access cards, USB drives, calculators, and wallets**, while transitioning staff away from physical paper logs.

## Updated Problem Statement
Campus students and administrative staff currently lack a single, shared system to report and track lost items, forcing them to rely on scattered social media channels and manual paper logbooks. This structural fragmentation resulted in 11 reported cases of severe time waste and 10 explicit complaints of information scattering, causing preventable financial loss from item replacements, anxiety, and an inefficient administrative workload for campus security.
